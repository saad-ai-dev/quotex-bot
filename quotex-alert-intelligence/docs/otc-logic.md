# OTC Market Analysis Logic

## What Are OTC Markets?

OTC (Over-The-Counter) markets on the Quotex platform are synthetic instruments that operate outside of regular market hours. Unlike LIVE markets which reflect real-world supply and demand from global exchanges, OTC instruments have prices generated by the platform's internal algorithms.

Key characteristics of OTC markets:
- Available 24/7, including weekends and holidays
- Prices are synthetically generated, not derived from real exchange data
- Price movements follow algorithmic patterns rather than fundamental market forces
- Spreads and volatility characteristics differ from LIVE markets
- Traditional technical analysis concepts have reduced (but not zero) applicability

## Why Separate Profiles?

The scoring engine uses separate OTC profiles because the nature of price generation is fundamentally different from LIVE markets.

### Reduced Reliability of Traditional Analysis

In LIVE markets, price action patterns, support/resistance levels, and market structure emerge from the aggregate behavior of millions of market participants. These patterns have been studied for decades and reflect genuine supply/demand dynamics.

In OTC markets, the price is generated algorithmically. While the algorithms often mimic real market behavior (creating candle patterns, respecting apparent levels), the underlying mechanics are different:

- **Support/Resistance**: Apparent S/R levels may be artifacts of the price algorithm rather than genuine accumulation/distribution zones
- **Market Structure**: Trend structures may be programmed cycles rather than genuine trend formation
- **Order Blocks**: Without real institutional order flow, order block concepts have limited theoretical basis
- **Volume**: Since there is no real volume, volume proxy analysis is less meaningful

### OTC-Specific Patterns

Despite the reduced applicability of traditional analysis, OTC markets exhibit their own detectable patterns:

#### 1. Cycle Detection

OTC price generators often exhibit cyclical behavior:
- Regular oscillation periods (e.g., bullish for N candles, then bearish for M candles)
- Cycle durations that remain relatively consistent within a session
- Phase shifts that can be tracked

The `otc_patterns` dimension specifically looks for:
```
cycle_length: Number of candles per complete bull/bear cycle
current_phase: Position within the current cycle (early, mid, late)
phase_direction: Whether the current phase favors bullish or bearish
cycle_confidence: How consistent the cycle has been recently
```

#### 2. Mean Reversion

OTC prices tend to revert to a rolling mean more predictably than LIVE markets:
- Deviation from the short-term moving average
- Rate of reversion after reaching extremes
- Overshoot patterns (price moves past the mean before settling)

#### 3. Pattern Repetition

The price algorithm may repeat recognizable sequences:
- Specific candle pattern sequences that recur
- Time-of-day patterns (some algorithms change behavior at certain intervals)
- Post-pattern behavior (what typically follows a specific pattern)

#### 4. Candle Sequence Analysis

Beyond individual patterns, the sequence of candle types carries information:
- Alternation patterns (bullish-bearish-bullish)
- Run lengths (consecutive same-direction candles before reversal)
- Transition probabilities (likelihood of the next candle direction given recent history)

#### 5. Anomaly Detection

Detecting when the OTC algorithm deviates from its normal behavior:
- Sudden volatility changes
- Pattern breaks in otherwise consistent cycles
- Unusual candle formations that do not match recent behavior

## Weight Distribution Rationale

### OTC 1m Profile

```json
{
  "otc_patterns": 25,
  "price_action": 18,
  "support_resistance": 12,
  "market_structure": 8,
  ...
}
```

- **otc_patterns (25)**: Highest weight because cycle detection is most actionable in short timeframes where cycles complete within the observation window.
- **price_action (18)**: Still relevant because the price algorithm generates recognizable candle patterns, and immediate pattern recognition provides timing information.
- **support_resistance (12)**: Reduced from LIVE (15) because apparent levels are algorithmic artifacts, but the algorithm does tend to create bounce-like behavior at certain prices.
- **market_structure (8)**: Significantly reduced from LIVE (12) because trend structure in OTC is largely synthetic.

### OTC 2m and 3m Profiles

As the expiry window increases:
- **otc_patterns** weight decreases slightly (22, 20) because longer windows span multiple cycles, reducing the directional clarity of cycle-based analysis.
- **market_structure** and **supply_demand** increase because the algorithm's larger-scale behavior becomes more relevant.
- **support_resistance** increases slightly as apparent levels have more time to produce bounce/rejection behavior.

## Threshold Differences

OTC profiles use lower thresholds than LIVE profiles:

| Parameter | LIVE | OTC | Rationale |
|-----------|------|-----|-----------|
| min_confidence | 60-65 | 55-60 | Lower because OTC analysis is inherently less certain |
| direction_margin | 15 | 12 | Smaller margin to avoid filtering out too many signals |
| min_bullish | 55 | 50 | Lower directional threshold |
| min_bearish | 55 | 50 | Lower directional threshold |

## Penalty Adjustments

| Penalty | LIVE | OTC | Rationale |
|---------|------|-----|-----------|
| conflict_weight | 1.2 | 1.0 | Conflicting signals are more common in OTC due to mixed analysis reliability |
| chop_weight | 1.0 | 0.8 | OTC markets are inherently choppier; penalizing less avoids filtering everything |
| weak_data_weight | 0.8 | 1.0 | Data quality is more critical for OTC pattern detection |
| timing_weight | 0.3 | 0.5 | Timing is more important for OTC because cycles can shift quickly |

## Limitations of OTC Analysis

Even with dedicated OTC analysis:

1. **Algorithm changes**: The platform may change its price generation algorithm at any time without notice, invalidating detected patterns.
2. **Non-stationarity**: Cycle lengths and patterns may shift during a session.
3. **No theoretical foundation**: Unlike LIVE market technical analysis, which has decades of academic and practitioner research, OTC pattern analysis is empirical and has no underlying market microstructure theory to support it.
4. **Overfitting risk**: Patterns detected in recent candles may be coincidental rather than systematic.
5. **Platform-specific**: All OTC analysis is specific to one platform's algorithm and does not generalize.

These limitations are reflected in the lower confidence thresholds and the explicit acknowledgment that OTC signals carry higher uncertainty.
